Are we still saying happy new year? 🫣 or counting to Valentine’s Day 👀 whatever the case - it’s 2026 and Céline & Lolo is our 1st review of what I believe will be a great foodie year ahead!

Celine & Lolo is located on Kabarsiran drive, and it actually used to be a very stunning white mansion before they turned it into an equally stunning boutique hotel.

First things first - DO NOT drive here. It’s a complete waste of time because there is absolutely no parking + they don’t let you park inside so if you don’t want your car just hanging loosely on that winding road - just Uber there, you’ll be glad you did.

I love what they did to the place in terms of decor and renovating it to a hotel. We got a nice peek at the rooms (the beds look comfy AF) and I love how much art surrounds the walls throughout the three floors before you get all the way downstairs to the poolside bistro.

{{<image-gallery key="celine" titles="celine-01 celine-02 celine-03">}}

I’m sure you’ve seen all the IG girlies make use of this sunny south of France-esque backdrop they have going on at the restaurant. I expected it to be bigger judging from the pics I saw but it’s a nice little spot with seating both indoors and outdoors.

I came here for a fun birthday lunch with my girl and we sat by the pool beds which was nice for the visuals but not great with that direct 30° January sun beating down on us 🥵 I needed hydration and caffeine asap so I ordered an iced caramel latte (400/) to start. I appreciate that they use [Spring Valley coffee]({{< relref "/spring-valley-cafe">}}) so the latte was exactly what the doctor ordered.

{{<image-gallery key="celine" titles="celine-04 celine-09">}}

The menu is quite simple, it’s a French style bistro so the food choices are minimal but very detailed in their execution. Got some bread and butter (KSH400) to ease my hunger as I waited for my date. You would think this would come out quite quick but my date arrived before the bread… this was about a 30 min wait. I asked what was taking so long - our server said the bread was being made fresh that’s why (after tasting said bread I don’t believe it but that’s their story and they’re sticking to it). That butter though?? That butter is what I believe was freshly made because it tasted superb! That last time I had butter that good was at [Fig & Olive]({{< relref "/fig-and-olive">}}) who also churn their own butter.

After stuffing myself with the ‘fresh’ bread & butter, we ordered some mains, my friend got the roasted chicken (KSH1700) and I got the red snapper (KSH1700) which is a dish that everyone’s been raving about. I had some fries (KSH500) too since my dish didn’t come with any accompaniments.

{{<image-gallery key="celine" titles="celine-05 celine-06 celine-07 celine-08">}}

The roast chicken comes with green peas and mashed potatoes while the red snapper is served on a bed of French beans (apt 😏) tomatoes & olives (I did not see any olives 👀). The chicken was super moist but according to my friend, it felt a tad undercooked… very slightly so but still. The mashed potatoes were divine I’m sure due to that glorious butter they have 🤤

The girlies were right about the red snapper - it slapped! It was perfectly crusted up top with a very tasty seasoning and was perfectly flakey on the inside. To some it may come off as salty but to me it was great. I should have gotten mashed potatoes instead of the fries because that fish + sautéed french beans + mashed potatoes is a perfect combo. The triple cooked fries are not worth it - very dry, they probably need to not triple cook them in my opinion 😬

Because coffee is anything but hydrating, I had to get another drink to cool me down. Tried a Shirley temple for the very first time and it was just what was needed on a sunny Saturday afternoon 🍒

{{<image-gallery key="celine" titles="celine-10 celine-11 celine-12">}}

The service here is quite slow so be wary of that but our server was very nice and I appreciated her candor 👌🏾 The ambience is 10/10 and that’s really what sells the place I feel - which isn’t ideal but I don’t think most people would visit here if they hadn’t seen the stunning pictures online because very few people talked about the food.

Would I come back? Not really. Maybe for drinks and a swim but not so much the food outside of that red snapper 😋 I would however recommend it for a staycation or for anyone visiting Nairobi - great place to stay and being in the Lavington area gives one proximity to so many other fun places in the city.
